Website Evaluations

Evaluating two websites using Contrast, Repetition, Alignment, and Proximity.

CRAP = Contrast, Repetition, Alignment, Proximity.

Apple Inc.

Apple’s homepage features bold product imagery, clear hierarchy, and consistent spacing. Everything feels intentional and polished.

  • Contrast:Excellent use of high contrast between text and background (black/white) and large, high-resolution imagery to draw attention. The typography hierarchy makes headings and CTAs stand out.
  • Repetition: Colors, fonts, and button styles repeat consistently across all sections and pages, reinforcing brand identity.
  • Alignment: Everything aligns on a precise grid. Text, buttons, and product images are centered or perfectly edge-aligned, creating a balanced layout.
  • Proximity: Related content is grouped tightly together, making it easy to understand relationships between elements.

LINGs CARS

Ling’s Cars is overloaded with clashing colors, moving animations, sound effects, and flashing banners. It’s memorable but overwhelming.

  • Contrast: Overuse of bright, saturated colors (neon pinks, greens, yellows) that compete for attention rather than guide it.
  • Repetition: Inconsistent fonts, colors, and button styles across the page. There’s no visual rhythm or predictable pattern.
  • Alignment: Everything feels thrown together, text boxes and images are misaligned, creating visual clutter and confusion.
  • Proximity: Unrelated elements are crammed together (ads, menus, cars, flashing GIFs) with no clear separation, making it hard to focus on any single task.
